RENUNCIATION TO HARMFUL PRACTICES

“Market Timing”

The Partner shall not resort to any practices that conflicts with the long-term interests of investors in a collective investment scheme. Such practices, known as “Market Timing” or “Short-Term Trading”, are characterised, in particular, by the fact that investors subscribe and redeem the same or a similar number of shares in a collective investment scheme within a short period of time, and/or that they seek to benefit from inefficiencies in the calculation of the net asset value.

The Partner undertakes to apply all reasonable measures, in compliance with applicable Laws (including, but not exclusively, CSSF Circular 04/146), to detect orders serving market timing purposes, reject such orders, inform the concerned investor of the reasons for such rejection, and train its sales force and operational teams on this topic. Pictet AM may, in any case, refuse to execute orders having been identified as serving market timing purposes.

“Late Trading”

The Partner acknowledges that, in accordance with the Operating Manual and the Funds’ Constitutional Documents, the TA accepts subscription, redemption and switch orders only until the cut-off time mentioned in the above mentioned documents and that orders received after such cut-off times shall be processed on the next valuation day. The same goes for subscription, redemption or switch orders received by the Partner before the cut-off time, but passed on to the TA after such cut-off time.
