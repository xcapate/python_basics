1.      DEFINITIONS

A.     “Confidential Information” refers to any fact, document or information that is not intended to be available in the public domain, especially:

a.      The amount of remunerations agreed in this Agreement;

b.      The composition of the Funds’ Portfolios;

c.      Information relating to the existing and/or prospective clients of the parties;

d.      Investment prospects, development strategies and all other information of a commercial nature.

B.     “Constitutional Documents” refers to the prospectus, the key investor information document (KI-ID), the articles of incorporation and/or the regulations of the relevant Fund.

C.     “CRS” refers to the Common Reporting Standard approved by the EICD Council on July 15, 2014.

D.     “Data Protection Framework” refers to all applicable European data protection and similar Personal Data and privacy laws, directives, regulations and rules including the Electronic Communications Data Protection Directive (2002/58/EC) and the EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016), as complemented by any additional national regulation requirements, national and/or EU authorities’ interpretations and guidelines of relevant rules, standard contractual and codes of practice issued by applicable regulatory bodies, as well as any relevant case law; for the purposes of this Agreement, the terms “data breach” (used interchangeably with “breach”), “processor”, “data subject”, “personal data”, “processing” and “controller” (each whether capitalised or not), as well as any other related terms, shall be interpreted pursuant to the Data Protection Framework”.

E.      “DDQ” refers to the Due Diligence Questionnaire that Pictet AM requires the Partner to fill in and update according to clause "Due Diligence".

F.      "FATCA” refers to the Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act.

G.     “Force Majeure Event” refers to a serious event which the party suffering it was not in a position to foresee, which is outside such party’s control and which such party is not in a position to overcome despite its diligence and efforts to resist, such as a natural cataclysm, armed conflict, substantial changes to the legal or tax environment or state injunction leading for instance to an embargo or to currency exchange restrictions.

H.     “FPC” refers to a contractual investment fund (Fonds de Placement Contractual) within the meaning of the Swiss Act of 23 June 2006 on Collective Investment Schemes, as amended from time to time.

I.       “Fund” refers to collective investment schemes created by the Pictet Group and covered by this Agreement, as detailed in Annex 2.

J.       "Law” refers to all laws, constitutions, treaties, ordinances, codes, regulations, directives, circulars and/or recommendations laid down by any government entity.

K.     “Management Company” refers to a management company within the meaning of Chapter 15 or 16 of the Luxembourg Law of 17 December 2010 on collective investment undertakings, as amended.

L.      “NAV” refers to the Net Asset Value per Share of each Fund.

M.    “Pictet Group” refers to Pictet & Cie Group SCA, in Geneva, as well as all companies and entities that are directly or indirectly affiliated thereto.

N.     "Positions” refers to the Shares held by the Partner, or the Partner’s clients, or on their behalf, by their custodian, representative or authorised nominee.

O.     “Shares” refers to shares or units in each of the Funds.

3.2.         Placement channels and commercial activity

A.     The Partner must inform Pictet AM on the placement channels it intends to use. Pictet AM may prohibit or restrict various placement channels.

B.     The Partner must obtain Pictet AM’s prior written consent to any changes in its placement channels and/or to the use of a new placement channel.

C.     The Partner informs Pictet AM of substantial changes to its business structure and/or its commercial activity.

D.     The Partners may act as nominee on behalf of its clients and in this case shall be bound by this Agreement, the Constitutional Documents and the applicable Laws.

3.3.         Provision of documentation

The Partner provides interested investors free of charge with the documentation provided by Pictet AM. If it also uses its own promotional materials as well as any other documents intended for publication, it must comply with the clause in this Agreement relating to promotional materials.

3.4.         Pictet AM’s assistance

Pictet AM provides the Partner with assistance in its activity and puts at its disposal the necessary documentation, in particular the Constitutional documents, annual and semi-annual reports, as well as any written or electronic information. 

4.      REMUNERATION AND EXPENSES

4.1.         Expenses

Every party bears the costs of performing its obligations hereunder. The Partner thus bears the costs of setting up, printing and shipping documents relating to the Funds other than those provided by Pictet AM, all costs of its promotional activities, as well as all fees due to its delegates or service providers.

5.      DELEGATION

5.1.         In general

A.     Subject to the provisions of Article "Sub-distribution/Placement', each party is authorised to delegate the execution of its duties hereunder to companies and entities that are affiliated to its group. 

B.     Delegation of contractual duties to third-party (unaffiliated) entities requires the prior consent of the other party.

C.     The delegating party remains fully liable to the other party of the execution of delegated tasks.

5.2.         Sub-distribution/Placement

The Partner must carry out its distribution/placement duties autonomously; it is not authorised to appoint other placement partners (“sub-distributors”).

6.      ADMINISTRATIVE PROCESSING

Administrative processes relating, in particular, to the opening of accounts and the placing and execution of Share subscription, redemption or conversion or transfer orders are described in the Operating Manual provided to the Partner at the latest at the signature of this Agreement and, for the rest, in the Funds’ Constitutional Documents.

7.      DOCUMENTATION REGARDING THE FUNDS

The Partner is not authorised to provide any other information than that contained in the documents provided by Pictet AM. In particular, it may not make promises or guarantees regarding the development of the Funds (performance, returns). If the Partner intends to issue its own documentation regarding the Funds, it undertakes to submit the corresponding documents to Pictet AM for prior approval, in all cases without exception.

8.      INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1.         Use of the Pictet AM logo and trademark

The Partner may in no case use the Pictet AM logos or the Pictet AM trademarks in its documents or materials of any kind (such as promotional documents, newspaper articles, Internet sites or links, headed paper, business cards, advertising banners linked for instance to sponsorship) in any manner without Pictet AM’s prior written consent. It is specified that Pictet AM’s consent shall only be in respect of the use being made of the Pictet AM logos or the Pictet AM trademarks, the Partner retaining sole responsibility for the contents and use of the document or support as a whole.

8.2.         Partner’s Internet site and search engine referencing

A.     If its Internet site mentions or refers to the Funds, the Partner must refer clearly, in its disclaimer relating to legal aspects, to the Constitutional Documents of the Funds and specify where these may be obtained. The Partner is solely responsible for its website. 

B.     The Partner undertakes not to seek to be referenced in association with the name “Pictet” on any search engine, without Pictet AM’s prior written approval. 

8.3.         REPRESENTATION AND CONFIRMATION OF THE PARTNER

A.     The Partner confirms that it holds any licence or authorisation required under applicable laws in the Territory to carry out its activity hereunder. It shall inform Pictet AM without delay in the event that such licence or authorisation is amended or withdrawn by the supervisory authority.

B.     The clause of this Agreement relating to immediate termination applies if the supervisory authority withdraws the required authorisation or licence.

C.     The Partner confirms that it is duly incorporated and has the powers and the authority required to sign and fulfil its obligations pursuant to this Agreement. 

D.     The Partner confirms that it complies with all of the Laws applicable throughout the Territory where it performs its activities.

E.      The Partner confirms that no legal or regulatory action is under way that affects it or a member of its group and that is likely to affect the performance of the services provided for under the terms of this Agreement. 

F.      The Partner accepts that it is considered an eligible counterparty within the meaning of Directive 2014/65/EU and therefore, the Partner is informed that certain protections provided for under Directive 2014/65/EU do not apply to it.

9.      FATCA AND CRS

A.     Each Party represents and warrants that it complies with FATCA and CRS. Pictet AM also hereby represents that the Funds comply with FATCA and CRS.

B.     In case a Party fails to comply with FATCA and this failure affects the FATCA status of the Funds as described in the respective Constitutional Documents or results in a withholding tax suffered by the Funds, the Parties will sign a specific addendum to settle the consequences deriving therefrom in good faith. If necessary, the Parties will re-evaluate further cooperation.

C.     The Partner will ensure that the relevant FATCA and CRS information is collected in relation to its clients investing in the Funds.

D.     The Parties agree to provide the counterparty to the extent permitted by law with such information, documentation and certifications as shall be required to enable each one of them to comply with FATCA and CRS.

10.   DUE DILIGENCE

The Partner undertakes to provide Pictet AM, upon request, any information regarding its organization (including risk and controls), its sales structure, and its activity in relation with the terms of this agreement. In particular, the Partner undertakes to provide on a regular basis to Pictet AM and upon request of the latter an updated DDQ. Pictet AM can outsource the process of collecting the DDQ and any supporting documents to a third party, in particular for digitization and automated processing.

11.   SALES RULES AND RESTRICTIONS

11.1.       Constitutional Documents and applicable laws

A.     The Partner shall carry out its activity in compliance with the applicable Constitutional Documents as well as any applicable laws in force within the Territory, in particular, European Directive 2014/65/EU regarding markets in financial instruments (MiFID II ) and its transposition into local legislation. 

B.     The Partner is responsible for supplying investors with, or making available to them, Constitutional Documents (notably the prospectus or the KIID) in accordance with the applicable legislation (in particular, pursuant to Article 80 (2) of Directive 2009/65/EC on the coordination of laws, regulations and administrative provisions relating to undertakings for collective investment in transferable securities). 

11.2.      Reporting

The Partner undertakes to provide on a regular basis Pictet AM with any information or documents related to the activity deployed by him under the Agreement, in compliance with the applicable Laws. The Partner also undertakes to provide Pictet AM, within 30 days of the end of each calendar quarter, a full and detailed report identifying all of the Positions in the Funds (including unpaid Positions) held by the Partner, or the Partner’s clients, or on their behalf by their custodian, representative or authorized nominee, according to the terms of this Agreement.

11.3.       Sales restrictions

A.     The Partner shall comply with sales restrictions set forth in the Funds’ Constitutional Documents or in applicable laws, as well as with those imposed by Pictet AM or other Pictet Group companies.

B.     The Partner’s attention is expressly drawn to the fact that only certain Funds, respectively certain Share classes, are authorised for sale in certain countries; the list of such authorisations is available at all times from Pictet AM. It is incumbent on the Partner to take these into account in order to carry out its activity hereunder in compliance with applicable rules relating to public distribution, private placement or any other form of commercialisation of fund shares in the Territory.

C.     In particular, Funds covered by this Agreement are not and will not be registered under the United States Securities Act 1933 as amended, and are not and will not be registered under the United States Investment Company Act 1940 as amended.  Consequently, the Partner acknowledges that the Funds may not be offered or sold, and represents that it has taken all adequate internal measures to ensure that the Funds shall not be offered or sold:  

a.      in the United States of America, nor in their territories and possessions; or 

b.      to or in favour of “US Persons”, as defined in Regulation S to the United States Securities Act 1933 as amended. 

D.     The Partner is prohibited from relying on any private placement exemption, or similar regime, that involves counting the aggregate number of offers of Shares made in a given jurisdiction.

E.      The Swiss Funds covered by this Agreement do not benefit from the passport provided by European Directive 2011/61/EU of 8 June 2011 on alternative investment fund managers (“AIFM Directive”) and there is no intent for it to be the case in the future; the Swiss Funds also do not comply with the private placement requirements set out by the AIFM Directive and there is no intent for it to be the case in the future. Consequently, the Partner acknowledges that the units of such Funds may not be marketed (as defined within the context of the AIFM Directive) to investors domiciled or having their registered office in the European union or in any other State where the AIFM Directive or similar provisions are in force; this applies even within the framework of a possible national private placement regime in force in such State.

12.   INVESTOR RELATIONSHIP MANAGEMENT

A.     The Partner must manage all relationships with investors stemming from their investments in the Funds.  

B.     In particular, the Partner shall be responsible for receiving any legally relevant investor statements relating to the Funds (queries, complaints, liability claims, requests for record consultation, etc.), for passing them on to Pictet AM without delay and for cooperating in settling rightful investor claims.

C.     Furthermore, the Partner confirms that it has taken note and understood the Constitutional Documents and acknowledges that it is solely responsible for assessing the suitability of an investment into the Funds by the investor, taking into account in particular to the investor’s knowledge and experience, financial situation, investment objectives, risk profile and tax situation.  

13.   PREVENTION OF MONEY LAUNDERING

A.     In relation to any applicable anti-corruption, anti-bribery, modern slavery, tax evasion or anti-money laundering and counter financing terrorism legislation (collectively, “Anti-Financial Crime Legislation”), the Partner will:

▹     Conduct its activities under this Agreement in accordance with, and not do or omit to do or permit anything to be done which is an offence or which may be deemed to be an offence under, the Anti-Financial Crime Legislation;

▹     Maintain appropriate policies, procedures and controls to prevent, detect and deter any form of bribery and corruption or money laundering or any other activity that would constitute a breach of Anti-Financial Crime Legislation in any of its activities under this Agreement; in particular, the Partner undertakes to (i) put in place and enforce a policy on the prevention of money laundering and the financing of terrorism and tax evasion, (ii) provide training courses to its staff, (iii) identify the source of the funds invested by its clients, (iv) carry out relevant AML screening and transaction monitoring, (v) establish and verify, by means of valid and official documents, the identity of investors and beneficial owners and the investors tax compliance.

B.     Upon reasonable request from the Pictet AM and/or any official body and to the extent permitted by local law, immediately provide Pictet AM or the official body submitting the request, with all the documents used to verify the person or entity's identity and/or the account opening documents for the said clients;    
         
Upon receiving a specific request, and subject to the legislation in force, to provide Pictet AM with the necessary documentation to allow the checks required by law (due diligence) to be carried out.

C.     This provision applies to the sub-distributors designated by the Partner, or to its clients, and the former undertakes to ensure that they comply with the said provisions. The Partner also undertakes to carry out any anti-money laundering due diligence as may be required with sub-distributors, in accordance with art. 5.2.B, fourth bullet.

14.   RENUNCIATION TO HARMFUL PRACTICES

14.1.       “Market Timing”

A.     The Partner shall not resort to any practices that conflicts with the long-term interests of investors in a collective investment scheme. Such practices, known as “Market Timing” or “Short-Term Trading”, are characterised, in particular, by the fact that investors subscribe and redeem the same or a similar number of shares in a collective investment scheme within a short period of time, and/or that they seek to benefit from inefficiencies in the calculation of the net asset value.

B.     The Partner undertakes to apply all reasonable measures, in compliance with applicable Laws (including, but not exclusively, CSSF Circular 04/146), to detect orders serving market timing purposes, reject such orders, inform the concerned investor of the reasons for such rejection, and train its sales force and operational teams on this topic. Pictet AM may, in any case, refuse to execute orders having been identified as serving market timing purposes.

14.2.       “Late Trading”

The Partner acknowledges that, in accordance with the Operating Manual and the Funds’ Constitutional Documents, the TA accepts subscription, redemption and switch orders only until the cut-off time mentioned in the above mentioned documents and that orders received after such cut-off times shall be processed on the next valuation day. The same goes for subscription, redemption or switch orders received by the Partner before the cut-off time, but passed on to the TA after such cut-off time.

15.   COST TRANSPARENCY

The Partner undertakes to comply with all applicable rules concerning cost transparency. The Partner must, in particular, ensure that it complies with any applicable laws and regulations requiring it to disclose to its clients the existence, nature, calculation basis and/or amount of the Trailer Fees received under this agreement and/or to repay such fees to them, in whole or in part.

16.   DILIGENCE AND LIABILITY

A.     Each of the parties undertakes to carry out its activity with the care and diligence one can expect from an expert.

B.     Each of the parties is liable to the other for any damages that may ensue from a breach, deliberate or as the result of negligence, of this Agreement or the law.

C.     The Partner shall indemnify Pictet AM for any damages or costs that may ensue from the Partner's failure to fulfil its obligations under all Laws regarding the distribution, marketing and sale of Units.

D.     Each of the parties is responsible for the actions of its employees, affiliates and agents in addition to their own actions.

17.   FORCE MAJEURE

A.     None of the parties may be held liable for any delay or default in the performance of any of its obligations hereunder if such delay or default results directly or indirectly from a Force Majeure Event.

B.     In the event of a Force Majeure Event, the party suffering it shall however only be exonerated from the affected obligation for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, with any other obligation remaining in full force; such party is required to inform the other party of the Force Majeure Event within fifteen days from the occurrence of such event, and to perform the affected obligation as soon as the Force Majeure Event ends.

C.     If the Force Majeure Event lasts for a period of more than thirty consecutive days, the parties shall consult within eight days following the expiration of such period to determine whether this Agreement should continue or end. In the event of a continuing disagreement at the end of the eight days, the party affected by the non-performance of the affected obligation shall be entitled to terminate this Agreement with immediate effect, within the meaning of article B.a of the Clause Duration and Termination.

Any amendment or addendum to this Agreement must be made in writing and requires the agreement of all parties.

24.   DURATION AND TERMINATION

A.     This Agreement is entered into for an unlimited duration. It may be terminated by each of the parties by registered mail, in whole or in respect of certain Funds and/or Share classes only, for the end of a quarter (March, June, September, December), with three months prior notice. In the event of a termination concerning certain Funds and/or Share classes only, this Agreement remains valid with respect to the Funds and Share classes not affected by the termination.

B.     This Agreement may also be terminated with immediate effect by each party, by way of exception from the ordinary notice period, by registered mail:

a.      in the event of a Force Majeure Event, at the conditions of article C of the Clause "Force Majeure";

b.      when one of the parties commits serious, or repeated despite prior warning, breaches of its duties hereunder.

C.     This Agreement is automatically terminated with immediate effect from the date when

a.      the supervisory authority withdraws one party’s licence or authorisation which such party was required to hold in order to carry out its duties hereunder;

b.      the Partner redeems its entire Positions, unless otherwise agreed between the parties;

c.      the Funds are liquidated and/or loose the authorisation or registration allowing them to be placed in the Territory in accordance with this Agreement. If certain Funds only are concerned by liquidation or by the loss of authorisation or registration, the Agreement is terminated in respect of such Funds only; it remains in force in relation to the other Funds.
